What Is a CBC Test and What Does It Measure?

A Complete Blood Count (CBC) is one of the most frequently ordered blood tests in clinical medicine. It measures red cells, white cells, haemoglobin, haematocrit, and platelets — the starting point for diagnosing anaemia, infection, bleeding disorders, and haematological malignancies. Modern automated analyzers report up to 25 parameters in minutes from a single EDTA tube.

HbA1c 20 Jan 2026

How Does an HbA1c Test Help in Diabetes Diagnosis and Management?

The HbA1c test reflects average blood glucose over 2–3 months by measuring the percentage of glycated haemoglobin — far more reliable than a single fasting glucose for long-term glycaemic monitoring. A value of 6.5% or above confirms diabetes; 5.7–6.4% indicates prediabetes. HPLC-based point-of-care analyzers deliver results in under 5 minutes while also detecting Indian haemoglobin variants (HbS, HbE) that interfere with other methods.

Hematology 3 Feb 2026

What Is the Difference Between a 3-Part and 5-Part Differential CBC Analyzer?

A 3-part differential groups WBCs into lymphocytes, monocytes, and granulocytes — compact, affordable, and sufficient for most primary care workflows. A 5-part differential uses laser-based flow cytometry to separate all five WBC subtypes, enabling detection of eosinophilia, left shifts, atypical lymphocytes, and early blast cells. The right choice depends on your patient mix, budget, and whether a reflex 5-part for flagged samples is practical.

Clinical Chemistry 17 Feb 2026

How Does a Clinical Chemistry Analyzer Work?

Clinical chemistry analyzers use spectrophotometry — measuring colour changes when reagents react with patient serum — to quantify liver enzymes, kidney markers, lipid profiles, glucose, and more from a single tube. Most modern platforms feature random-access processing, running multiple tests simultaneously, with throughput from 100 to over 1,000 tests per hour. Selecting the right tier requires matching daily test volume, menu breadth, and reagent economics to your workflow.

Immunoassay 4 Mar 2026

What Is Immunoassay Testing and When Is It Used in Diagnostics?

Immunoassay uses the specific binding of antibodies to detect substances at nanogram or picogram concentrations — covering thyroid hormones, cardiac Troponin, reproductive markers, tumour markers, and infectious disease panels such as Hepatitis B, dengue NS1, and HIV. Automated chemiluminescence and fluorescence platforms deliver quantitative results in 3–15 minutes, replacing manual ELISA in medium and large Indian laboratories.

Blood Gas 18 Mar 2026

What Is Blood Gas Analysis and Why Is It Critical in ICU Settings?

Arterial Blood Gas (ABG) analysis measures pO2, pCO2, pH, bicarbonate, and base excess — an immediate window into a patient's ventilatory status and acid-base balance. In critical care, a 30-minute lab delay can change ventilator settings or alter resuscitation decisions. Bedside cartridge-based analyzers now deliver complete ABG results in under 2 minutes from a single heparinised syringe, eliminating the need for a centralised blood gas lab.

Coagulation 1 Apr 2026

What Is a Coagulation Analyzer and What Tests Does It Run?

Coagulation analyzers evaluate the haemostasis system by measuring clotting time — running PT/INR (warfarin monitoring, liver assessment), APTT (heparin monitoring, haemophilia screening), Fibrinogen, Thrombin Time, and D-Dimer for DVT and DIC. Both optical (turbidimetric) and electromechanical detection methods are used. Reliable PT/APTT results in under 3 minutes are non-negotiable in high-volume cardiac surgery, dialysis, and obstetric settings.

Clinical Chemistry 22 Apr 2026

What Is an Electrolyte Analyzer and Why Are Sodium and Potassium So Critical?

Electrolyte analyzers use ion-selective electrodes (ISEs) to measure Na+, K+, Cl−, Ca2+, and other key ions in under 90 seconds from just 150 μL of sample. Potassium imbalance is among the most immediately life-threatening electrolyte disorders — hyperkalaemia in renal failure can trigger fatal cardiac arrest with little warning. Dedicated ISE analyzers are indispensable in ICUs, emergency departments, and dialysis centres where real-time values drive decisions.
